Albania
  • Landscape: Very mountainous country, especially in the north and east (Albanian Alps). The coast (Adriatic and Ionian Sea) is flatter with Mediterranean vegetation. Central Albania is more hilly and rural.
  • Street View Coverage:Mostly around main highways and cities like Tirana, Durrës, Shkodër, and some tourist roads. Many rural roads still not covered.
  • Road Signage:
    • Highways (Autostrada): Green signs, sometimes marked with "A" (e.g., A1, A2, A3).
    • National roads (Rrugë kombëtare): Blue signs, labeled "SH" + number (e.g., SH2, SH3, SH4).
    • Secondary roads: Smaller, often poorly signed or not signed at all, especially in rural areas.

Austria
  • Landscape: Very mountainous in the west and south (Alps), with alpine meadows, forests, and snowy peaks. The east (Vienna region, Burgenland) is flatter and more agricultural. Many scenic river valleys (like the Danube), lakes, and historic towns.
  • Street View Coverage: Official Google Street View is limited. Exists only in selected cities like Vienna, Graz, Linz, Salzburg, and some nearby towns. Most countryside, mountain roads, and small towns are not covered. Imagery is from around 2010–2012 and hasn't been updated.
  • Road Signage:
    • Motorways (Autobahnen): Green signs, labeled "A" + number (e.g., A1, A2). Toll roads requiring a "Vignette" (sticker).
    • Expressways (Schnellstraßen): Labeled "S" + number (e.g., S6, S36). Often look like motorways, may also require a vignette.
    • Federal Roads (Bundesstraßen): Blue signs, labeled "B" + number (e.g., B1, B311). Important regional roads.
    • State Roads:
      • "L" Roads: Landesstraßen – lower-tier regional roads managed by states. Signs may be white with black text or just small markers (e.g., L123).
      • "K" Roads: Gemeindestraßen or Kreisstraßen – local or municipal roads. Marked with "K" + number (e.g., K15), but signage is minimal or absent. Often seen in rural areas, villages, and backroads.
    • City streets: Named as "[Name]straße" (e.g., Mozartstraße), "Gasse" for small streets, "Platz" for squares.
    • European roads (E-roads): Co-signed with A/S/B roads. White on green (e.g., E60, E66).
Belgium
  • Landscape: Flat coastal plains in Flanders, rolling hills and forests (Ardennes) in Wallonia. Major rivers like the Scheldt and Meuse traverse the country.
  • Street View Coverage: Excellent nationwide. All motorways, regional roads, towns and villages are covered and frequently updated.
  • Road Signage:
    • Motorways (Autosnelwegen / Autoroutes): Green signs, labeled "A" + number (e.g. A1, A15). White text.
    • National roads (N-roads): White signs with red border, labeled "N" + number (e.g. N4, N90).
    • Regional / Provincial roads: Blue signs, often labeled with "R" + number around ring roads (e.g. R0 Brussels ring).
    • Local streets: Named on white plates with black text, often "Straat / Rue" followed by name.
    • European routes (E-roads): White on green, co-signed with A or N roads (e.g. E19, E42).
Bulgaria
  • Landscape: Mountainous in the south and west (Rila, Pirin, Rhodope ranges), flatter in the north and east. Danube forms the northern border, and the east has a coastline on the Black Sea.
  • Street View Coverage: Widespread coverage on main and secondary roads, including cities and tourist areas. Rural villages might be less covered.
  • Road Signage:
    • Motorways: Green signs, labeled "A" + number (e.g., A1).
    • National roads: Blue signs, labeled "I" or "I" roads (e.g. I-5, II-63) or without "II/I" just a number.
    • City streets: "ulitsa ["name"]" (улица or ул.) is used for "street" in Cyrillic. or "Blvd ["name"]" (бул.) in Cyrillic.
Croatia
  • Landscape: Very diverse – mountains along the coast (Dinaric Alps), flat plains in the east (Slavonia), and a long Adriatic coastline with many islands.
  • Street View Coverage: Full coverage including small islands, rural roads, and tourist hotspots. Very good in cities like Zagreb, Split, Dubrovnik.
  • Road Signage:
    • Highways: Green signs, labeled "A" + number (e.g., A1, A3). Toll roads.
    • State roads: Blue signs, labeled "D" + number (e.g., D8).
    • City streets use "Ulica [name]" or just "[name] St."
Czech Republic
  • Landscape: Mountains along borders (Krkonoše, Šumava), rolling highlands (Český les, Vysočina), and lowland plains (Polabí, Morava).
  • Street View Coverage: Good. Introduced mid-2010s; covers most city streets and major roads, though some minor country lanes may be missing.
  • Road Signage:
    • Motorways (Dálnice): Green signs, labeled "D" + number (e.g. D1, D5). Toll system via vignette.
    • High-capacity roads (Rychlostní silnice): Former "R" roads (e.g. R4) now reclassified, signage varies.
    • First-class roads: Blue signs, labeled "I/" + number (e.g. I/35).
    • Second-class roads: Blue signs, labeled "II/" + number (e.g. II/150).
    • Third-class roads: Blue signs, labeled "III/" + number.
    • Local roads: Often unsigned or small white markers; in towns, named "ulice [Name]" or "náměstí [Name]".
    • European routes: Co-signed with D or I on green background, labeled "E" + number (e.g. E55).
Denmark
  • Landscape: Flat islands and peninsulas, coastal dunes, polders, and some rolling hills in the southeast (Møns Klint).
  • Street View Coverage: Complete and up-to-date. All public roads, villages and many cycle paths covered.
  • Road Signage:
    • Motorways (Motorvej): blue signs, "E" + number (E20, E45) or no prefix; overhead gantries common.
    • Motortrafikvej (limited-access main roads): blue signs, "M" + number (M1, M4).
    • Primary regional routes: white signs with red border, "Rute" + number (e.g. Rute 21).
    • Local roads: small white plates "L" + number; rural lanes often unsigned.
    • European routes: co-signed with E on blue or green backgrounds.
    • City streets: "Vej" (street/road), "Gade" (street), "Plads" (square), "Allé" (avenue).
Estonia
  • Landscape: Pine forests, extensive bogs and lakes, low limestone cliffs on the Baltic coast, plus many islands (Saaremaa, Hiiumaa).
  • Street View Coverage: Complete nationwide since 2013. Main roads, secondary routes and most rural lanes are covered.
  • Road Signage:
    • Main roads (Põhimaantee): blue signs labeled "T" + number (T1…T11).
    • Secondary/state roads (Tugimaantee): blue signs with two-digit numbers (e.g. 15, 92) without prefix.
    • Municipal/local: often unsigned or small white plates, sometimes "M" + number.
    • European routes: white on green, "E" + number (e.g. E67).
    • City streets: "Tänav" (street), "Puiestee" (avenue), "Plats" (square).
Finland
  • Landscape: Thousands of lakes and forests dominate, flat coastal plains in the south, rolling hills in the middle, and fells and tundra in Lapland.
  • Street View Coverage: Very good on main highways (Valtatie), regional roads (Kantatie), and many local roads. Coverage around all cities (Helsinki, Turku, Tampere, Oulu, Rovaniemi).
  • Road Signage:
    • Main highways (Valtatie): Blue signs, "Vt" + number (e.g. Vt4, formerly numbered I).
    • Regional roads (Kantatie): Blue signs, "Kt" + number (e.g. Kt51).
    • Connecting roads (Yhdystie): White signs, "Yt" + number.
    • Local roads: Often small white plates or unsigned, labeled "tie" + number or just name.
    • City streets: "[Name] katu" (street), "tie" (road), "aukio" (square).
    • European routes: Co-signed on green, "E" + number (e.g. E8, E75).
France
  • Landscape: Very diverse. Mountains in the southeast (Alps), southwest (Pyrenees), central (Massif Central), and east (Vosges). Flat plains and farmland dominate the north and west. Coastal regions vary: Atlantic coast has dunes and pine forests, Mediterranean coast is dry and hilly. Northern areas often have cloudy skies and stone villages; southern regions are sunnier and have more Mediterranean architecture.
  • Street View Coverage: Excellent. Full coverage of nearly all roads, including rural villages, mountain passes, and even hiking paths in some places. Some city centers may be blurry due to privacy laws (esp. Paris), but Street View is frequently updated.
  • Road Signage:
    • Autoroutes (A): Blue signs, white text, labeled "A" + number (e.g., A6, A75). Toll roads. Very high quality. Often have rest stops ("Aire") and signage for "péage" (toll station).
    • National roads (N): Red signs, white text, labeled "N" or "RN" + number (e.g., N7, RN20). Formerly main routes before motorways existed. Still used heavily.
    • Départementales (D): Yellow signs, black text. Labeled "D" + number (e.g., D23, D1000). Extremely common – even tiny rural roads have D-numbers. May be narrow or winding depending on location.
    • Communal roads (C): Local roads managed by communes. Usually unsigned or marked with "C" + number on small signs. Often very narrow, especially in rural areas.
    • Local naming conventions:
      • Route: Generic for any rural road. E.g., "Route de Paris".
      • Rue: Urban street. E.g., "Rue Victor Hugo", "Rue de la République".
      • Avenue: Wider or major street, often tree-lined. E.g., "Avenue Charles de Gaulle".
      • Boulevard: Big urban roads, often in city rings. E.g., "Boulevard Haussmann".
      • Chemin: Small rural road or path. E.g., "Chemin du Moulin". Can be gravel or paved.
      • Voie: Often used for urban residential or service roads. E.g., "Voie des Écoles".
      • Impasse: Dead-end street. E.g., "Impasse du Château".
    • European Roads (E-roads): Co-signed with A or N routes, labeled "E" + number (e.g., E15, E60). Green signs with white text.
Germany
  • Landscape: Flat North German Plain, forested low hills in central Germany (Harz, Thuringian Forest), and the Alps in the south (Bavaria).
  • Street View Coverage: Excellent. Coverage since around 2008 on almost all public roads, including rural lanes and city streets.
  • Road Signage:
    • Autobahns: Blue signs, labeled "A" + number (e.g. A3, A9). No universal speed limit.
    • Federal roads (Bundesstraßen): Yellow signs with black text, labeled "B" + number (e.g. B1, B27).
    • State roads (Landesstraßen): White signs with black text, labeled "L" + number (e.g. L123). Managed by Länder.
    • District roads (Kreisstraßen): White signs with black text, labeled "K" + number (e.g. K45). Local importance.
    • City streets: Named "Straße" or "Weg" or "Platz" (e.g. Bahnhofstraße, Lindenweg, Marktplatz).
    • European routes: Co-signed with A or B on green background, labeled "E" + number (e.g. E40, E45).

Hungary
  • Landscape: Mostly flat to gently rolling terrain. The Great Hungarian Plain (Alföld) dominates the east and south – wide open fields, farmlands, few trees. Western Hungary and around Lake Balaton is more hilly. Northern Hungary has some low mountains like the Bükk and Mátra ranges. Forested areas and vineyards appear especially in the north and west.
  • Street View Coverage: Very good across the entire country. Almost all cities, towns, and even many villages have coverage. Good coverage of highways, secondary roads, and even some rural dirt roads. First added in 2013 and regularly updated.
  • Road Signage:
    • Motorways (Autópálya): Green signs, labeled with "M" + number (e.g., M1, M3, M7). Toll roads. E-road numbers (e.g., E60) often co-signed.
    • Main roads: Blue signs, labeled with 1–2 digit numbers (e.g., 6, 44), sometimes with yellow shields for expressways.
    • Secondary roads: White signs with black borders, labeled with 3+ digit numbers (e.g., 441, 338).
    • City streets: Often named "[Name] utca" (street), "[Name] tér" (square), "[Name] körút" (ring road), "[Name] út" (road), or "[Name] sugárút" (avenue). Example: "Petőfi utca", "Kossuth Lajos körút".
    • Direction signs are often rectangular and very standardized, with destinations and road numbers clearly marked.
  • Unique Features:
    • Language: Hungarian (Magyar) – completely unique language. Common words on signs: *község* (village), *város* (town), *utca* (street), *híd* (bridge), *tér* (square).
    • Lots of directional signs showing town names ending in *-falva*, *-szent*, *-háza*, etc.
Iceland
  • Landscape: Volcanic with glaciers (Vatnajökull), lava fields, black sand deserts, geothermal areas, fjords in the west & north, and the Ring Road circling the island.
  • Street View Coverage: Good along the Ring Road (Route 1) and around major towns (Reykjavík, Akureyri, Egilsstaðir). Many secondary roads and interior highland tracks are not covered.
  • Road Signage:
    • Main ring road (Þjóðvegur 1): Blue signs, "1".
    • Primary roads (Þjóðvegur): Yellow signs, "Vt" + number (e.g. Vt 50).
    • Secondary roads (Öðru stigarvegir): White signs, "F" + number (mountain roads require 4×4) or plain numbers for paved roads (e.g. 22).
    • Local roads: Often unsigned gravel tracks labeled "vegur" or just number.
    • City streets: "gata" (street) or named after landmarks.
Ireland
  • Landscape: Rolling green hills and farmland, rugged west coast cliffs, boglands in the midlands, low mountains (Wicklow, Mourne), and scenic lakes (Killarney, Lough Neagh).
  • Street View Coverage: Comprehensive nationwide coverage since around 2012. Motorways, national roads, regional roads, and most local lanes are covered.
  • Road Signage:
    • Motorways: Blue signs, "M" + number (e.g. M1, M50). Toll plazas on some routes.
    • National Primary: Green signs, "N" + number (e.g. N7, N20). Main inter-urban routes, often dual carriageway.
    • National Secondary: White signs with green panel background for number, "N" + three digits (e.g. N104).
    • Regional roads: White signs, "R" + three digits (e.g. R125). Connecting towns and villages.
    • Local roads: Often unsigned or marked with local names only.
    • City streets: "Street", "Road", "Avenue", "Lane", "Place", "Square". Bilingual signage usually not used.
    • European routes: Occasionally co-signed, white on green (e.g. E01).
  • Driving Side: Left-hand traffic.
Italy
  • Landscape: Extremely diverse. The north features the Alps – high mountains, snowy in winter, with winding roads and lakes (Como, Garda). Central Italy (Tuscany, Umbria) is hilly with vineyards, olive groves, and historic stone villages. The south (Calabria, Apulia) is drier, warmer, with more Mediterranean vegetation. Islands like Sardinia and Sicily have their own dry, mountainous terrain.
  • Street View Coverage: Very extensive and high-quality. Almost full country coverage including remote rural roads, mountains, and even pedestrian-only historical areas in major cities (like Rome, Florence, Venice). Frequently updated.
  • Road Signage:
    • Motorways (Autostrade): Green signs, labeled with "A" + number (e.g., A1, A4). Usually toll roads. High-quality roads with good markings.
    • State Roads (Strade Statali): Blue signs, labeled "SS" + number (e.g., SS16, SS1 Via Aurelia).
    • Provincial Roads (Strade Provinciali): White signs with black text, labeled "SP" + number (e.g., SP7).
    • City Streets: Typically use names like "Via [name]" (street), "Viale" (main road/avenue), "Piazza" (square), "Corso" (main boulevard). Examples: "Via Roma", "Piazza Garibaldi", "Corso Italia".
    • European Roads (E-roads): Often co-signed with other roads, shown in white text on green background (e.g., E35).
Latvia
  • Landscape: Flat coastal plains, mixed forest and lakes (Gauja), gentle hills in Vidzeme, agricultural lowlands in Zemgale.
  • Street View Coverage: Very good since 2013. All A-roads, many regional (P-roads), and most rural lanes are covered.
  • Road Signage:
    • State main roads (Valsts galvenie autoceļi): green signs, "A" + number (A1 Riga–Ainaži).
    • State regional roads: white signs, "P" + number (P80, P109).
    • Local roads: small white plates, sometimes "V" + number.
    • European routes: white on green, "E" + number (E67 "Via Baltica").
    • City streets: "iela" (street), "prospekts" (avenue), "laukums" (square), "ceļš" (road).
Lithuania
  • Landscape: Flat to gently rolling, with forests, lakes (Dūkštai, Trakai) and Baltic dunes; agricultural plains in central and southern regions.
  • Street View Coverage: Excellent—full coverage of all A-roads, most secondary and many local roads since 2012.
  • Road Signage:
    • Magistralės (A-roads): green signs, "A" + number (A1 Vilnius–Kaunas, A18 Vilnius beltway).
    • National roads: yellow signs, just number (1–99) on red border (e.g. 51).
    • Regional roads (Krašto keliai): white signs with "KK" + number (e.g. KK150).
    • Local/municipal: often unsigned or small plates "V" + number (vietinės reikšmės), varies.
    • European routes: white on green, "E" + number (e.g. E272).
    • City streets: "Gatvė" (street), "Prospektas" (avenue), "Skveras" (square), "Kelias" (road).
Luxembourg
  • Landscape: Hilly and forested in the north (Oesling / Ardennes), rolling plateaus in the center, the Gutland region in the south is more agricultural.
  • Street View Coverage: Very good. Main roads, villages, and even many minor country lanes are covered.
  • Road Signage:
    • Motorways (Autoroutes): Green signs, labeled "A" + number (e.g. A1, A7). Toll-free.
    • Main national roads: Blue signs, labeled "N" + number (e.g. N4, N13).
    • Regional / local roads (Chemins / Wege): White signs with black text, labeled "CR" + number (Chemin Repris) or simply "W" + number in German speaking areas.
    • City streets: White plates with black text, "Rue", "Avenue", "Weg", "Platz".
    • European routes (E-roads): Co-signed with A or N roads, white on green (e.g. E44).
Montenegro
  • Landscape: Very mountainous country, especially in the north and east (Dinaric Alps). The coastline along the Adriatic Sea is rocky but flatter, with beautiful coastal towns. Central areas are hilly to mountainous, with deep river canyons (like Tara or Moraca).
  • Street View Coverage: Mostly around main roads, cities like Podgorica, Nikšić, and coastal towns such as Kotor, Budva, and Bar. Good coverage along E-roads and major tourist routes. Many rural roads are still uncovered.
  • Road Signage:
    • Main roads: Blue signs, sometimes labeled with "M" (e.g., M2, M2.3, M3).
    • Regional roads: Usually marked with "P" + number (e.g., P19).
    • European roads (E-roads) often signed as E65, E80 etc., typically white on green or blue backgrounds.
    • In cities, Street View roads are typically named "Ulica [something]" ("Ulica" = street). Sometimes just "[name] St." on English-language maps.
Netherlands
  • Landscape: Very flat, extensive polders, canals and dikes, low coastal dunes in the west and north. Some gentle hills in the southeast (Limburg).
  • Street View Coverage: Complete and up-to-date. All major and minor roads, villages and cycle paths are covered extensively.
  • Road Signage:
    • Motorways (Autosnelwegen): Blue signs, labeled "A" + number (e.g. A2, A12). White text.
    • National roads (Rijkswegen): Blue signs, labeled "N" + number (e.g. N325).
    • Provincial roads: Blue signs, labeled "N" + three or four digits (e.g. N257).
    • Local streets: White plates with black text, "Straat", "Weg", "Laan", "Dreef", etc.
    • European routes (E-roads): Co-signed with A roads, white on green (e.g. E25).
North Macedonia
  • Landscape: Mostly mountainous and rugged, with the Šar Mountains in the northwest and long valleys like the Vardar Valley cutting through the center. Lake Ohrid in the southwest is a major natural and tourist spot. Central and eastern parts are more hilly and dry.
  • Street View Coverage: Good coverage since 2019, especially along major roads and in cities like Skopje, Bitola, Ohrid, Tetovo, and Prilep. Many rural roads are covered, but some remote areas may still be missing.
  • Road Signage:
    • Motorways: Green signs, labeled "A" + number (e.g., A1, A2). Usually modern signage.
    • National roads: Blue signs, labeled "M" + number (e.g., M4, M5), though some are being reclassified to "R".
    • City streets often named using "Ulica [name]" (улица = street).
    • European roads (E-roads) are also signed (e.g., E-75, E-65), usually on green or white backgrounds.
Norway
  • Landscape: Extremely varied – fjords and steep mountains along the west coast, rolling hills and forests inland, Arctic tundra in the north (Lofoten, Finnmark), and flat river valleys in the southeast.
  • Street View Coverage: Very good along national routes (Rv- and E-roads) and around major towns (Oslo, Bergen, Trondheim, Tromsø). Many smaller county roads and scenic mountain passes are covered, though very remote tracks may be missing.
  • Road Signage:
    • European routes: Green signs, "E" + number (e.g. E6, E39).
    • National roads (Riksvei): Red-on-white signs, "Rv" + number (e.g. Rv15).
    • County roads (Fylkesvei): Blue signs, "Fv" + number (e.g. Fv305). Often narrower.
    • Local roads and farm tracks: Sometimes unsigned or small white plates, occasionally labeled "vei" (road) or "grusvei" (gravel road).
    • City streets: Usually named "[Name] gate" (street) or "vei".
Poland
  • Landscape: Varied – flat Baltic coast and lakes in the north (Masuria), rolling central plains, forested uplands (Kashubia, Świętokrzyskie), and the Carpathian and Sudeten mountains in the south.
  • Street View Coverage: Very good nationwide. Introduced around 2010 and regularly updated; most roads, towns and even many rural lanes are covered.
  • Road Signage:
    • Motorways (Autostrady): Blue signs, labeled "A" + number (e.g. A1, A4). Toll roads.
    • Expressways (Drogi ekspresowe): Green signs, labeled "S" + number (e.g. S7, S8). High-speed dual carriageways, may have some at-grade intersections.
    • National roads (Drogi krajowe): White signs with black text and red border, labeled "DK" + number (e.g. DK10, DK94).
    • Voivodeship roads (Drogi wojewódzkie): White signs with black text, labeled "DW" + number (e.g. DW782). Regional importance.
    • County & Municipal roads: Often unsigned or small local numbering; "droga powiatowa/gminna" rarely appears on Street View signs.
    • City streets: Named "ul. [Name]" (ulica = street), "pl. [Name]" (plac = square), "al. [Name]" (aleja = avenue).
    • European routes: Co-signed with A/S/DK roads on green background, labeled "E" + number (e.g. E75, E30).
Portugal
  • Landscape: Very varied – the north is mountainous (Serra da Estrela, Peneda-Gerês), the Douro valley is terraced vineyards, central Portugal (Beiras, Ribatejo) is rolling hills and plains, the Alentejo is flat and cork–oak country, the Algarve coast is rocky cliffs and sandy beaches. Don't forget the islands: Madeira (volcanic mountains, laurisilva forest) and the Azores (nine volcanic islands with crater lakes, high cliffs).
  • Street View Coverage: Excellent nationwide—mainland and islands. All motorways, principal routes, national roads and most secondary and rural roads are covered and regularly updated. Urban centres (Lisbon, Porto, Faro, Funchal) have dense coverage.
  • Road Signage:
    • Motorways (Autoestradas): Green signs, labelled "A" + number (e.g., A1 Lisbon–Porto, A2 Lisbon–Algarve). Toll roads ("Portagem") with electronic toll lanes (Via Verde) and toll plazas.
    • Principal Itineraries (Itinerários Principais): Green signs, labelled "IP" + number (e.g., IP1 Lisbon–Porto coastal alternate, IP5 to Azores ferry docks).
    • Complementary Itineraries (Itinerários Complementares): Green signs, labelled "IC" + number (e.g., IC19 to Sintra, IC6 between Abrantes–Santarém).
    • National Roads (Estradas Nacionais): White signs with red border, labelled "N" + number (e.g., N1, N125 Algarve coastal road).
    • Azores Regional Roads (Estradas Regionais): Blue or white signs, labelled "ER" + number (e.g., ER2-1 on São Miguel, ER1 on Terceira).
    • Madeira "Fast Roads" (Via Rápida): Blue signs, labelled "VR" + number (e.g., VR1 around Funchal, VR2 on Porto Santo).
    • Municipal Roads (Estradas Municipais): Often unsigned or marked on small plates "EM" + number (e.g., EM548). Quality varies—usually narrow local lanes.
    • Urban Streets: Named with "Rua" (street), "Avenida" (avenue), "Largo" (square), "Travessa" (alley), "Calçada" (cobbled street). Examples: "Rua de Santa Catarina" (Porto), "Avenida da Liberdade" (Lisbon).
    • European Routes (E-roads): Co-signed in green, labelled "E" + number (e.g., E1 north–south, E80 across north coast).

Serbia
  • Landscape: Mostly flat in the north (Vojvodina region), with large agricultural plains and the Danube river. The central and southern parts are hilly to mountainous, with ranges like Kopaonik, Zlatibor, and Tara. Serbia has many river valleys and forested areas.
  • Street View Coverage: Available nationwide since around 2020. Full coverage in urban areas (Belgrade, Novi Sad, Niš), decent in towns and rural roads. Many touristic and scenic mountain routes are also covered.
  • Road Signage:
    • Highways (Autoput): Green signs, labeled "A" + number (e.g., A1, A2). Many are also part of E-routes (E70, E75). Toll roads.
    • National roads: Blue signs, labeled "IB" class or older "M" system (e.g., M21, IB-12).
    • Regional/local roads: Yellow or white signs, sometimes poorly marked in rural zones.
    • City streets usually use "Ulica [name]" (Улица) – means "street" in Serbian (Cyrillic or Latin depending on the region).
  • Unique Features:
    • Dual script: Both Latin and Cyrillic used on signs – especially in rural areas and official signage.
    • Concrete utility poles with Y- or T-shaped tops are common in rural areas.
    • Landscape can change quickly between flat agricultural plains and forested hills.
Slovakia
  • Landscape: Mountains (Tatras, Fatra) in north, hilly central highlands, flat Danubian plains in south.
  • Street View Coverage: Good since 2014. Major roads, towns and many rural lanes are covered, though some remote mountain paths may be missing.
  • Road Signage:
    • Motorways (Diaľnice): blue signs, "D" + number (D1, D2). Toll via sticker.
    • Expressways (Rýchlostné cesty): green signs, "R" + number (R1, R4).
    • Primary roads: blue signs, "I/" + number (I/50).
    • Secondary: blue signs, "II/" + number; tertiary "III/".
    • European routes: white on green, "E" + number (E58, E75).
    • City streets: "Ulica" (street), "Námestie" (square), "Cesta" (road).
Slovenia
  • Landscape: Mostly mountainous and hilly terrain. Alps in the northwest, forested hills in the center, and flatter terrain in the east. Small coastline on the Adriatic Sea.
  • Street View Coverage: Full nationwide coverage, including small villages and mountain roads. Very high quality, updated frequently.
  • Road Signage:
    • Highways: Green signs, labeled "A" + number (e.g., A1, A2). Toll system via vignette.
    • Expressways: Marked "H" + number (e.g., H4), also green signs.
    • Regional roads: Blue signs, often labeled with "R" or just numbers.
    • City streets use "Ulica [name]" or mostly "[name] cesta / c.".
Spain
  • Landscape: Ultra‐diverse – from the green, rainy NW (Galicia, Asturias) through rolling central Meseta (castilian plains and plateaus), Mediterranean hills & coasts (Catalonia, Valencia, Murcia), the arid southeast (Almería), to Alpine peaks in the Pyrenees and Sierra Nevada. Atlantic coast features cliffs & dunes; islands (Balearics, Canaries) add beaches and volcanic terrain.
  • Street View Coverage: Excellent nationwide. All motorways, autovías, national roads and nearly all regional & even many local roads have Street View. Urban centers and rural backroads alike are covered and updated regularly.
  • Road Signage:
    • Autopistas (toll motorways): Blue signs, "AP-" + number (e.g. AP-7, AP-46).
    • Autovías (free dual-carriageway): Blue signs, "A-" + number (e.g. A-1, A-92).
    • National roads (Carreteras Nacionales): White signs with red border, "N-" or "CN-" + number (e.g. N-340, CN-232).
    • European routes (E-roads): Co-signed on green background, "E" + number (e.g. E-15, E-90).
    • Regional/Autonomous Community roads: Vary by region – prefix + number, typically on white or yellow signs:
      • Andalucía: "A" (provincial A-roads) and "CA-" (Cádiz), "GR-" (Granada), "SE-" (Sevilla), etc.
      • Aragón: "A" (Autovías) vs. provincial "Z-" (Zaragoza), "TE-" (Teruel), "HU-" (Huesca).
      • Asturias: "AS-" + number.
      • Balearic Islands: "PM-" + number.
      • Canary Islands: "TF-" (Tenerife), "GC-" (Gran Canaria), plus "TF-" on La Palma, etc.
      • Cantabria: "CA-" + number.
      • Castilla y León: Provincial codes (see list below) like "BU-" (Burgos), "LE-" (León), "SO-" (Soria), etc.
      • Castilla-La Mancha: "CM-" for regional trunk roads; provincial codes like "TO-" (Toledo), "CU-" (Cuenca).
      • Catalonia: "C-" + number for main; "GI-", "B-", "T-", "L-", "SS-" etc. per province.
      • Valencian Community: "CV-" + number; "V-" for Valencia province local roads.
      • Extremadura: "EX-" + number.
      • Galicia: "AC-" (A Coruña), "LU-" (Lugo), "OR-" (Ourense), "PO-" (Pontevedra).
      • Madrid: "M-" + number for regional roads.
      • Murcia: "RM-" + number.
      • Navarra: "NA-" + number.
      • Basque Country: "BI-" (Bizkaia), "SS-" (Gipuzkoa), "A-" (Araba) – note "A-" can collide with national A-autovías.
      • La Rioja: "LR-" + number.
      • Ceuta & Melilla: "CE-" and "ML-" + number.

Sweden
  • Landscape: Flat to gently rolling plains in the south, forests and lakes in the central regions, rocky highlands and tundra in the north (Lapland), and rocky archipelagos on both coasts.
  • Street View Coverage: Excellent on all motorways (E-roads), primary (Rv), and most county roads (Lv). Many smaller rural roads are covered as well.
  • Road Signage:
    • European routes: Green signs, "E" + number (e.g. E4, E20).
    • National roads (Riksväg): Blue signs, "Rv" + number (e.g. Rv40).
    • County roads (Länsväg): White signs with green text, "Lv" + number (e.g. Lv832).
    • Local roads: Often unsigned or small white plates labeled "väg" + number or just name.
    • City streets: "[Name] gata" (street), "väg", "torg" (square).
Switzerland
  • Landscape: Extremely mountainous – the Alps dominate the south and east, with dramatic valleys, snowy peaks, and glacial lakes. The central region (Mittelland) is more hilly and populated. The Jura mountains in the northwest are lower. Scenic villages, lakes, and forests are common across the country.
  • Street View Coverage: Limited and partially outdated due to strict privacy laws. Available mainly in cities (Zurich, Bern, Geneva, Basel) and some major roads and tourist spots. Coverage from 2009–2011. Most rural areas, small villages, and mountain roads lack Street View.
  • Road Signage:
    • Motorways (Autobahnen / Autoroutes / Autostrade): Green signs, labeled "A" + number (e.g., A1, A2). Toll-free but require a vignette. Well-maintained with many tunnels.
    • Main roads: Blue signs with just a number (e.g., 1, 11, 9). Used for interregional routes and sometimes scenic alternatives to motorways.
    • Kantonal roads (Kantonsstrassen): Often unsigned or marked with white/black signage, labeled "K" + number (e.g., K21). Local to each canton. Quality varies from wide paved roads to narrow alpine passes.
    • Local roads and paths:
      • Weg: Common suffix meaning path, trail, or small rural lane. Examples: "Bergweg", "Waldweg", "Talweg". Can be paved or gravel, and sometimes accessible by car, bike, or only on foot.
    • City Streets:
      • German-speaking areas: "[Name]strasse", "Hauptstrasse", "Bahnhofplatz", or "Weg".
      • French-speaking areas: "Rue de [name]", "Avenue", "Place".
      • Italian-speaking: "Via", "Piazza", "Strada".
    • European Roads: Co-signed with A-roads, white text on green background (e.g., E35).
Turkey
  • Landscape: Extremely diverse. Western and southern coasts (Aegean & Mediterranean) are green and hilly with olive trees and Mediterranean vegetation. The central Anatolian plateau is dry, flat or rolling, with more desert-like appearance (especially around Konya). Eastern Turkey is very mountainous with deep valleys and snowy peaks. The Black Sea coast (north) is lush, rainy, and mountainous.
  • Street View Coverage: Partial. Good coverage in western cities (Istanbul, Ankara, İzmir), touristic coastal regions, and major highways. Central and eastern Turkey have spotty or no coverage. Many rural roads and smaller towns lack official Street View. Major E-roads and intercity highways are usually covered.
  • Road Signage:
    • Motorways (Otoyol): Green signs, labeled with "O" + number (e.g., O-1, O-3, O-21). Toll roads. High quality and well signed.
    • State roads (Devlet Yolu): Blue signs with "D" + number (e.g., D100, D650). Most common for intercity travel.
    • Provincial roads (İl Yolu): Usually white or yellow signs, with codes like 06-54 (first part = province code).
    • City streets: Often labeled with "Cadde" (avenue) or "Sokak" (street). "Bulvarı" = boulevard. e.g., "Atatürk Caddesi", "Barış Sokak".
    • European roads (E-roads): Often co-signed with Turkish roads (e.g., E80 = D100). Signed with white letters on green background.
Ukraine
  • Landscape: Mostly flat to gently rolling plains, large agricultural areas, forested regions in the west (Carpathian Mountains), and the Crimean peninsula with coastal cliffs and steppe areas.
  • Street View Coverage: Official Street View introduced in 2016, coverage mostly along main highways, major cities like Kyiv, Lviv, Odesa, Kharkiv, and some tourist routes. Rural areas and minor roads are often not covered or have outdated imagery.
  • Road Signage:
    • International Roads (M): Blue signs, "M" + number (e.g., M06, M03). These are the main highways connecting major cities and borders.
    • National Roads (H): Yellow signs, "H" + number (e.g., H01, H08). Important national routes but generally smaller than M roads.
    • Regional Roads (P): White signs with black "P" + number (e.g., P32). Connect smaller towns and regional centers.
    • Local Roads (T): White signs with "T" + number, usually minor or rural roads.
    • City Streets: Named streets often end with "Street" (vulytsia), "Avenue" (prospekt), "Lane" (provulok), or "Square" (ploshcha). Names are usually in Ukrainian with Latin transliterations on maps.
United Kingdom
  • Landscape: Very varied – high moorland and mountains in Scotland (Highlands), rolling hills in northern England (Pennines), lowlands and farmland in eastern England, rugged coastlines in Wales and southwest England (Cornwall), and flat fenlands in East Anglia.
  • Street View Coverage: Excellent and up-to-date nationwide. All motorways, A-roads, B-roads, urban streets and most rural lanes are covered since around 2008–2010.
  • Road Signage:
    • Motorways: Blue signs, "M" + number (e.g. M1, M25). Junction numbers on small white panels.
    • Primary A-roads: Green signs, "A" + number (e.g. A1, A40). Many dual-carriageway sections.
    • Non-primary A-roads: White signs, "A" + number (e.g. A259). Single carriageway rural roads or urban connectors.
    • B-roads: White signs, "B" + number (e.g. B4367). Local importance, often narrow.
    • Unclassified roads: Often unsigned or small white signs with local names.
    • Trunk roads in Scotland. Some "A"-roads carry "Trunk" status, marked by red outline on signs (e.g. Trunk A82).
    • City streets: "Road", "Street", "Avenue", "Lane", "Close", "Drive", "Court".
    • European routes: Rarely signposted (e.g. E15 in some regions), white on green.
  • Driving Side: Left-hand traffic.
